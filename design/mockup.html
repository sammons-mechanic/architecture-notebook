api.acme.com

The public HTTPS entrypoint for the Acme trading API. TLS termination, JWT enforcement, and the route to the Order Engine behind it.

Public HTTPS entry point for the Acme trading API, terminating TLS at an application load balancer in Production AWS and forwarding traffic to the Order Engine service. All inbound requests must carry a JWT issued by our Auth0 tenant; unauthenticated requests are short-circuited at the listener rule before reaching application code.

The certificate is rotated automatically by ACM with DNS-01 validation against the parent zone. Domain delegation comes from acme.com, which also fronts the customer portal and admin dashboard — see those entries for the corresponding ingress definitions. Rate limits are enforced at the WAF, keyed on the bearer subject claim; offenders receive 429 and surface in the cloud account access log.

5.1.1.1.1.1Egress from this ingress

Behind the ALB, traffic enters a private subnet and reaches the Order Engine task on port 8080 over the cluster service mesh. Outbound calls from that service — to Plaid, Stripe, and the internal ledger — are documented under Order Engine and its associated Plaid webhook entry.

If you are reviewing this ingress for a security audit, the JWT verification flow and the Plaid HMAC verification are the two control points most often cited. Follow the references on this page to walk both.
